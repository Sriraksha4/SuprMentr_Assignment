1. Features Used in Spam Detection
The system can look at several features, such as:

- Presence of suspicious words like "Free", "Win", "Offer", "Congratulations"
- Number of links in the email/message
- Whether the sender is known or unknown
- Too many capital letters
- Too many special symbols
- Message length
- Repeated words

These features help the model understand whether a message looks suspicious.

2. Data Needed
To train a spam detection system, we need:

- A dataset of spam messages
- A dataset of non-spam (normal) messages
- Labels showing whether each message is spam or not

The more examples the system sees, the better it can learn patterns.

3. Possible Mistakes
A spam classifier can make two major mistakes:

False Positive
A normal and important email may be wrongly marked as spam.
Example: A college placement email goes to the spam folder.

False Negative
A spam message may be wrongly marked as normal.
Example: A fake lottery message appears in the inbox.
